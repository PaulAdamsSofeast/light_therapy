☢️
UV Light — Medical Use Only. UV-B (280–315nm) and UV-A (315–380nm) carry ionising radiation risk. Therapeutic UV is used under clinical supervision for psoriasis, vitiligo, eczema, and jaundice. Home UV exposure without proper dosing and eye/skin protection can cause DNA damage, burns, and increased skin cancer risk. Do not self-treat with UV devices without medical guidance.

Key principles to remember:
1. Wavelength = penetration depth = what gets treated. Shorter wavelengths (blue, violet) stay at the skin surface. Longer wavelengths (NIR) reach deep muscle, joints, and nerves.
2. Green light is unique — its pain effects work neurologically via the eyes, not photobiomodulation through skin. You need to look at it, not shine it on tissue.
3. UV requires medical supervision. The therapeutic window for safe home use starts at visible blue (415nm).
4. Best all-round consumer combination: 660nm + 850nm. Covers skin and deep tissue in one panel. Add 415nm for acne, 670nm for eyes/brain, full-spectrum white for SAD.
5. Avoid the 700–770nm dead zone. No meaningful therapeutic activity.
